About Me

Scholarly Biography

I am a senior lecturer in Civil Engineering at the School of Architecture, Technology and Engineering. My expertise lies in the fields of transportation and transport externalities. I published many peer-reviewed articles on topics such as transport modelling, transport impact on environment and health, transport and land-use interaction, transport and energy efficiency, fleet modelling and air quality modelling.

I have considerable industrial experience in the fields of transportation planning, modelling and engineering. I worked on research projects such as Persuasive Trip Advisor for Reducing CO2 consumption (EU FP7-ICT), Emission Inventory Compilation (EPA, Ireland), and Carbon Neutrality (EPA, Ireland). In the industry, I worked on the Transport Modelling Framework and Public Transport Framework of the National Transport Authority, Ireland, Bus Rapid Transit and Bus Network Restructuring projects of Dhaka Transport Coordination Authority, Bangladesh.

Software Expertise: Transportation: Aimsun, VISSIM, TransCAD, COPERT, Junctions10; Layout Design: AutoCAD; Data Modelling: MATLAB, R, SPSS, ArcGIS, Visual Basic Environment (Excel-based).

Knowledge Exchange

I find the connections between teaching with industry and research are very important to provide students with diverse perspectives. I often challenge students with examples from my industrial and research experience. Working with different transport models under the Transport Modelling Framework of the National Transport Authority in Ireland is useful for that.

Sharing my experience from transport engineering and planning activities that I undertook in Bangladesh while working on the District Town Infrastructure project, Bridge feasibility project, and the Bus Rapid Transit project is also useful for knowledge exchange. Experience of working as a researcher on many projects provided me with excellent experience to deal with the topics of sustainable and advanced transportation systems.

I am also a recognised reviewer of international journals such as Transport Research Part D, Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, Atmospheric Environment, and International Journal of Sustainable Transportation. Reviewing articles is an excellent activity that stimulates me to harness new knowledge and makes me aware of errors that I am interested in sharing with students.

Research Interests

My research addresses issues of smart mobility and future transport, exploring fleet modernization using energy-efficient vehicles and connected or autonomous vehicles, activity-based models, mobility as a service, and their impact on the environment, climate change, health, and land use pattern.
